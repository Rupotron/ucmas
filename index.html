About UCMAS

More than number skills.

UCMAS focuses on child development, not just arithmetic. The program helps children develop basic cognitive skills and cognitive executive functions by combining the abacus with guided math practice.

Cognitive skills help children think, read, learn, remember, reason, pay attention, process information, compare, analyze, and understand cause and effect. With practice and the right training, these skills can improve over time.

FAQs

Common questions.

What is UCMAS?

UCMAS is a child development program that uses Math as a medium and Abacus as a tool to develop cognitive skills and mental arithmetic ability.

Is UCMAS only about math?

No. UCMAS is positioned as much more than number skills. It develops cognitive skills such as memory, reasoning, attention, observation, imagination, and confidence.

What techniques are used?

The program may include perception training, speed writing, flash cards, listening exercises, basic exercises, abacus practice, and visual arithmetic.

What does COMIC JARS mean?

It refers to Concentration, Observation, Memory, Imagination, Creativity, Judgment, Application, Reasoning, and Self Confidence.
